Yo there, not sure how you stumbled up here, but happy to have ya !! A lot has
happened last year, and it was one heck of a mental ride where I experienced many
different kinds of emotions in a cute little amount of time.

## Ambitious Beginning
I was in my final weeks of my internship (dates back to Aug 2020) and had applied
to Google (India) off-campus, which from my past experience gives about 10 days time to write
a test after receiving the link which itself takes about 3+ weeks to recieve, but
to my surprise I received a test link within 2 days and had to take the test in 4 days.
And you guessed it right, I nuked it :) , to top it off my internship was also kinda
demanding (an early stage startup). I wasn't that sad, because I knew Google would
pay a visit back again during our placements and honestly the questions were also
tough to solve with just 6 days of practice.

<!-- --- -->

## Anxious First Wave
Thus began my placements, I had neatly prepared my resume, with 2 internships, one
being at an early stage startup, Goldcast and other at a prestigious open source
program called Google Summer of Code, technically I had 8 months of experience writing
 production level code and have done projects on wide range of topics like Securities,
Deep Learning etc, had everything you would expect from a good developer, everything
but a good GPA, it was 6.01 that too in a non-CS branch, even then I was confident
that I would atleast get shortlisted to write the tests. Life had different plans
for me, being from Aerospace, of the 400+ SDE job openings, I got to apply for only
80 openings, and among the 80 only 15 profiles shortlisted me for the test, vast
majority had a cutoff of 7 GPA. I was already getting crushed with the limited
job openings but the cutoffs take the trophy for shaterring my confidence. This partly
affected my preparation, making me think what am I even preparing for.

<!-- --- -->

## Depressing Second Wave
It was not going good but wasn't bad either. Atleast the test of shortlisted profiles
were going great, acheived pefect score on tests of Amazon, Paypal and SAP, and had
very good scores on many other tests. I had my expectations still up, until,
Goolge (India) came. So this time let alone nailing the test, I was not even shortlisted
for it. If that wasn't enough, none of Amazon, Paypal or SAP shortlisted me for
the interview despite acheiving perfect score in their tests. At this point (2 weeks before interviews)
I had neither the hope nor the confidence left in me. My mind wasn't even supporting
to revise the topics which I already knew.

I will be continuing my interview experience in the second part of the blog.
